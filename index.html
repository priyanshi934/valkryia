The Scale of the Crisis

of women with PCOS go undiagnosed their entire lives.

India is in the middle of a silent hormonal health crisis. And no one is talking about it.

Nearly 1 in 5 young Indian women has PCOS. Symptoms are normalised for years. Doctors are undertrained. The internet is flooded with misinformation. And there is zero government policy. Valkryia exists because someone had to build this.

01 — Medical System
Doctors aren't equipped
Most Indian physicians prescribe only "lose weight" — unaware of insulin resistance, evidence-based dietary approaches, or the mental health dimension. Only 9.1% of patients learned anything from their doctor after diagnosis.
PGIMER Chandigarh, 2021
02 — Digital Health
Existing apps don't work
Commercially built PCOS apps score lowest on engagement and evidence quality. Built to look good, not to help. No clinical oversight, no real personalisation, and zero accountability when they get it wrong.
JMIR mHealth Analysis, 2024
03 — Information
The internet is full of lies
Influencers with 12-week certifications sell expensive "root cause" fixes, demonize proven treatments, and push restrictive diets — making women feel their symptoms are personal failures.
BBC World Service, Sep 2024
04 — Policy
Zero government action
There is a near-total absence of government-led awareness initiatives for PCOS in India. No national strategy. No guidelines. The burden has been entirely offloaded onto a fragmented private sector.
IMPRI Policy Research, 2025
